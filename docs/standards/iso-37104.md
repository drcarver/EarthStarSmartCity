# ISO 37104 – Sustainable Cities and Communities  
**Framework for Integrating City Systems**  
**Genesis Network Implementation Reference**

**ISO 37104:2019** provides guidance for integrating urban systems to achieve sustainable, resilient, and smart cities. It emphasizes holistic interconnection of infrastructure, services, and governance to optimize performance, reduce silos, and enhance adaptability. The Genesis Network fully aligns with **ISO 37104** by designing regenerative hubs as interconnected, bidirectional systems using **[smart ceramic pipes](../components/smart-ceramic-pipes.md)**, **[DC microgrids](../components/dc-microgrids.md)**, and **[restoration zones](../components/restoration-zones.md)**.

## 1. Core Principles of ISO 37104

- **System Integration**: Connect physical (infrastructure), digital (data/IoT), and social (governance/community) layers.  
- **Bidirectional Flow**: Enable resource sharing (energy, water, data, materials) across systems.  
- **Maturity Progression**: From fragmented to fully integrated and adaptive cities.  
- **Key Focus Areas**: Energy, water, waste, transport, buildings, ICT, governance.  

Genesis targets **advanced integration maturity** (equivalent to Level 4–5 in complementary standards) by 2035.

## 3. Bidirectional Infrastructure in Genesis

Central to **ISO 37104** compliance is the **[smart ceramic pipes](../components/smart-ceramic-pipes.md)** network enabling:
- Export/import of surplus power/water/biogas between pods/hubs/affiliates  
- Slurry piping for manure/digestate → centralized **[biogas digesters](../components/biogas-digesters.md)**  
- Data/fiber conduits for **[data moat](../docs/data-moat.md)** integration  
- Pneumatic tubes for material/food transport  

This creates a truly integrated city system, exceeding baseline interoperability requirements.
