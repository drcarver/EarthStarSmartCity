# NASA-STD-3001  
**Human Spaceflight – Environmental Control and Life Support Systems (ECLSS)**

**NASA-STD-3001** (Volume 2) defines technical requirements for **closed-loop life support** in human spaceflight — air revitalization, water recovery, waste management, and food production.

Genesis Network adapts **NASA-STD-3001** principles for terrestrial regenerative hubs.

## Key Requirements & Genesis Mapping

| NASA-STD-3001 Section              | Requirement                                         | Genesis Implementation                                                                 | Notes |
|------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------|
| Air Revitalization                 | CO₂ removal & O₂ generation                         | Biogas CO₂ enrichment; **aquaponics** oxygen production                                | 95–99% closure |
| Water Recovery                     | >90% wastewater recycle                             | **AWG**, **digestate recovery**, condensate collection                                 | Matches or exceeds |
| Waste Management                   | Resource recovery from waste                        | **Plasma vitrification**, **BSFL conversion**, **biorefinery**                         | Circular materials |
| Food Production                    | Bioregenerative systems                             | **Multi-trophic aquaponics**, **dense ag**, **microbial inoculants**                   | MyPlate compliance |
| Monitoring & Control               | Real-time environmental monitoring                  | **Hyper-dense sensors** + **RISC-V edge AI**                                            | 3–5× redundancy |

**Feedback Loop**  
Earth hub data (PB-scale) refines **ECLSS** parameters → improves NASA lunar/Mars designs.
