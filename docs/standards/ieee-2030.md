# IEEE 2030 – Smart Grid Interoperability Framework  
**Genesis Network Implementation Guide**  
**Last Updated: January 22, 2026**

## Overview

**IEEE 2030** is the foundational standard for **smart grid interoperability** adopted across the Genesis Network. Published by the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE), it provides a comprehensive framework for integrating **energy systems**, **information technology**, and **communication networks** in a secure, reliable, and scalable manner.

In the Genesis Network, **IEEE 2030** ensures that all **DC-dominant microgrids**, **bidirectional smart pipes**, **piezo/solar roads**, **BIPV glazing**, **energy storage** (sodium-ion batteries), and **distributed generation** (biogas microturbines, agrivoltaics) operate seamlessly across hubs, affiliates, and future regenerative cities.

## Core Principles of IEEE 2030 in Genesis

1. **Interoperability Architecture**  
   The standard defines three architectural layers that Genesis fully implements:  
   - **Power Systems** → physical generation, transmission, distribution, and end-use  
   - **Information Technology** → data models, protocols, and cybersecurity  
   - **Communication Networks** → wired/wireless media, latency, bandwidth  

2. **Key Interfaces Covered**  
   Genesis prioritizes these high-impact interfaces:  
   - **Power ↔ IT**: Metering, demand response, distributed energy resource (DER) control  
   - **IT ↔ Communication**: Secure data exchange (MQTT, CoAP, OPC UA)  
   - **Power ↔ Communication**: Real-time monitoring of **piezo elements**, **BIPV output**, and **inductive charging lanes**  

3. **Genesis-Specific Adaptations**  
   - **DC-native grid** (avoids AC conversion losses) → compliant via IEEE 2030.1 (EV infrastructure) and IEEE 2030.5 (DER communication)  
   - **Private land microgrids** → bypasses traditional utility regulations while maintaining IEEE 2030 interoperability  
   - **Faraday mesh + ceramic shielding** → enhances electromagnetic resilience beyond baseline requirements

## Compliance & Certification Path

- **Baseline**: All Genesis smart infrastructure meets or exceeds **IEEE 2030** core requirements  
- **Extensions**: Selective adoption of IEEE 2030.5 (DER), IEEE 2030.7 (microgrid controllers), IEEE 2030.9 (energy storage)  
- **Testing**: Digital twin simulations + physical prototypes in pilot hubs  
- **Audits**: Annual third-party verification aligned with **ISO/TC 268** smart city KPIs  

## Benefits to Genesis Network

- Enables seamless integration of **distributed energy resources** (DER) across thousands of hubs  
- Supports **bidirectional flow** of power, water, data, and slurry  
- Provides **cybersecurity baseline** against EMP, hacking, or physical threats  
- Facilitates **NATO-aligned** and **global adoption** by meeting recognized international standards
