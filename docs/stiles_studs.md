## Building Structure and Materials

### Enhanced Ceramic Tiles
The facility uses **171,750 Enhanced Ceramic Tiles** (5 m² each, $10/tile, 200 kg) for the facade, floors (10–15 cm thick), foundation (50 cm thick), and windows. Tiles are fiber-reinforced composites:
- **Composition**: 60% silicon carbide (SiC), 25% aluminum oxide (Al₂O₃), 10% graphene/CNTs, 5% zirconia (ZrO₂).
- **Properties**: 2,800 MPa compressive strength, 700–900 MPa flexural strength, 20–30 MPa·m¹/² fracture toughness, >95% recyclability via supercritical CO₂ and waste-to-energy.
- **Manufacturing**: In-house graphene/CNT synthesis (CVD, 900–1,000°C, methane feedstock, >90% purity), hot isostatic pressing (HIP, 1,600–1,800°C, 80–150 MPa), microchannels (0.5 mm) for fluid flow.
- **Features**:
  - **Photovoltaic Cells**: 28.75% efficiency, 200–240 W/m², generating 101.82 MW (with geothermal heat pumps).
  - **Piezoelectric Sensors**: 0.5–2 W/m² for structural monitoring.
  - **Thermoelectric Generators**: 1–3 W/m² for waste heat recovery.
  - **Atmospheric Water Generation (AWG)**: 0.5 L/m²/day, 80–90% humidity capture, yielding 85,875 L/day.
  - **LFP Batteries**: 0.5 kWh/tile, 85.875 MWh total storage.
  - **IoT Sensors**: 100/tile, AES-256 encryption, monitoring air, water, and structural health.
  - **EMP Shielding**: Copper grounding mesh, >80 dB attenuation.
- **Window Tiles**: Transparent ceramic (spinel, AlON) with PV (15% efficiency), AWG, and sensors, maintaining structural and functional equivalence.
- **Stealth Tiles**: For military applications, incorporating radar-absorbing materials (RAM, e.g., iron-doped SiC) and thermal signature reduction (thermoelectric cooling), compliant with MIL-STD-461G.

Tiles snap together using **DARPA’s BlockADE** modular construction system, enabling rapid assembly and replacement (1,718 tiles/year, 1% failure rate, $150/tile replacement: $100 recycling, $50 robotic labor).
