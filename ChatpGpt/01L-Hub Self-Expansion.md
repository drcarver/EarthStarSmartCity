## Core Principle

**Every mature Genesis hub functions as a self-replicating factory cluster**, producing all 8 essential pods on-site.  

This ensures:  
- Exponential network growth (doubling every 18–24 months)  
- Independence from external suppliers after initial bootstrap  
- Cash-positive replication without supply-chain bottlenecks  

---

## Replication Timeline

| Phase | Months | Key Actions |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| **Phase 1 – Bootstrap** | 1–6 | Pre-supply pods delivered (8–16). Tile & Pipe Production Pod activated for early cash flow; no pod replication yet. |
| **Phase 2 – Closure Ramp** | 7–12 | Tile factory matures (10k–50k m²/mo). Mini-fab components arrive. Begin on-site assembly of duplicate Tile & Pipe Pods. Surplus funds expansion. |
| **Phase 3 – Self-Replication Threshold** | 13–18 | Hub reaches 95% closure, $200–800M/yr run-rate. Produces first **full set of 8 pods** on-site. Dedicate 10–20% output for daughter hubs. |
| **Phase 4 – Exponential Growth** | 18+ | Mature hubs become **mother factories**. Replication: 1–2 new hubs every 12–18 months. Network doubles every 18–24 months internally. |

- **Replication Cost per Hub**: $140–320M, 70–90% offset via on-site production  
- **Time to Mature Daughter Hub**: 12–18 months  
- **Network Growth Rate**: Doubles every 18–24 months with 10–20 mother hubs  
- **Strategic Uplift**: +20–30% viability by removing external supply risk

Hub self-replication is **the engine of the Genesis exponential model**.  

By prioritizing **Tile & Pipe Production Pods** and mini-fabs early, the network ensures:  
- True independence from external pod deliveries  
- Rapid, scalable global expansion  
- Cash-positive, supply-chain-resilient operations
