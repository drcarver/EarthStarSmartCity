# Microbial Inoculants  
**Production, Application, and Integration in the Genesis Network**

**Microbial inoculants** are living cultures of beneficial microorganisms (bacteria, fungi, archaea, microalgae) applied to soil, plants, aquaponics systems, livestock feed, and waste streams to enhance nutrient cycling, stress tolerance, yield, disease suppression, and sequestration. In the **Genesis Network**, they are a critical **viability enhancement**, delivering **10–30% average yield increases** across trophic levels while accelerating soil regeneration and closed-loop efficiency.

## 2. Production Pod Specifications

**Microbial Inoculant Production Pods** are dedicated 40 ft high-cube ceramic-lined units with:

- Fermenters (10–500 L scale, pH/DO/temp control)  
- Centrifuges & freeze-drying for concentration/stabilization  
- Sterile filling lines for liquid/powder formulations  
- Sensor array (**hyper-dense monitoring**) for contamination detection  
- Edge AI for real-time strain optimization via **[data moat](../docs/data-moat.md)**  

**Input Sources**: Digestate, farm residues, waste streams, **[RHA silica](../components/tiles-catalog.md)** (carrier).  
**Materials**: Toughened ceramics, stainless internals, HEPA filtration.  
**Cost**: $50–100K per pod (mature scale).  
**Revenue**: $100–300K/year per pod (sales to affiliates, premium pricing).

## 3. Application Methods

- **Soil Drench / Seed Coating**: Liquid or powder formulations applied pre-planting or via drip irrigation  
- **Foliar Spray**: Diluted live cultures for leaf microbiome enhancement  
- **Aquaponics Media**: Direct addition to grow beds/fish tanks  
- **Livestock Feed**: Fermented silage or direct probiotic inclusion  
- **Waste Streams**: Inoculation of manure/digestate for accelerated biogas production
