# Exterior Cladding Tile  
**Genesis Network Tile Family – Vertical Facade Application**

## Overview

**Exterior Cladding** tiles form the primary vertical surface layer of most **Genesis pods**, **housing modules**, **community buildings**, and **regenerative city structures**. They provide structural protection, supplementary power generation, passive water harvesting, thermal regulation, and cultural/aesthetic expression while remaining **95–100% waste-derived** at maturity.

These tiles are designed to integrate seamlessly with the **snap-together modular system** and connect to embedded conduits for **bidirectional power**, **water**, **data**, and **pneumatic lines**.

## Manufacturing Process

1. **Raw Material Intake** — Primarily **C&D slag** (60%), rice husk ash silica, farm residues, ELFM slag  
2. **Mixing & Forming** — High-shear mixers + hydraulic presses / extruders  
3. **Drying** — Tunnel dryers with waste heat recovery  
4. **BIPV & Sensor Embedding** — Thin-film PV application + sensor insertion during green state  
5. **Firing** — Tunnel kilns 1,200–1,400°C (biogas or electric)  
6. **Finishing** — Glazing / patterning / quality inspection (vision-guided robotics)  
7. **Packaging** — Flat-pack pallets for kit distribution  

All production occurs in **Tile Production Pods** or clustered permanent factories (post-2030 trigger).

## Input Sources & Circularity

- **Primary** — Construction & Demolition (C&D) waste slag (60%)  
- **Secondary** — Rice husk ash (silica source), sugarcane bagasse/straw/grape pomace (pore-formers)  
- **Tertiary** — ELFM-mined landfill slag, brownfield residues  
- **Circularity** — 70–95% waste-derived at maturity; internal slag from plasma vitrification reused
