# Biogas Digester Pod

The Biogas Digester Pod is a core energy production module in the Genesis Network, converting organic waste (farm residues, manure, food scraps) into methane biogas for power generation and digestate fertilizer for closed-loop agriculture. Launched in Phase 1 (2027–2028), it provides baseload renewable energy, supports network autonomy, and generates revenue through surplus export, tipping fees, and fertilizer sales. By 2040s, each pod contributes $1.5–$5M/year in value, with network-wide revenue of $4–$15B/year from energy and byproducts.

## Viability & Integration Notes

The Biogas Digester Pod is highly viable (8.5/10), providing resilient baseload energy with low CAPEX ($0.8–2.5M) and strong ROI (1–2 years) through revenue from power export ($1–3M/year) and digestate fertilizer ($0.5–2M/year). It integrates seamlessly with waste-generating pods (aquaponics, livestock) for full closure, uses sensor data for predictive optimization, and supports smart city standards via automated compliance. Smart airlocks in all structures scan for weapons/explosives, alerting security via mmWave/hyperspectral systems.
