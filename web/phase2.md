# Genesis Network: Scale Phase (2028–2029)

## Phase Overview

The **Scale Phase** (2028–2029) marks the transition from bootstrapping to regional dominance, achieving **85–95% material closure**, permanent production facilities, municipal waste contracts, and rapid affiliate expansion. With 10–50 hubs and 1,000–5,000 affiliates operational, the system demonstrates full self-funding while launching premium branding, infrastructure upgrades, and the standalone **TerraWell FreeCare System** as a humanitarian layer.

Key priorities:
- Permanent ceramic tile factories and scaled plasma vitrification.
- ELFM/sewage pilots replacing local systems.
- Aggressive bidding on infrastructure upgrades (roads, rest stops, dams) using surplus recycled feedstock.
- Full affiliate subsidies (power $0–0.05/kWh, low tipping fees, microbial kits).
- TerraWell pilots providing free care/goods with strict firewall.

## Core Actions

### Production & Closure
- **Permanent Factories**: Build ceramic facilities on remediated brownfields; output 500–800M m² tiles/year network-wide.
- **ELFM/Sewage Integration**: Mine landfills for slag/metals; process municipal sludge into biogas/struvite; secure first replacement contracts.
- **Translucent Tile Production**: Dedicated lines for greenhouse/field enclosures, enabling 2–4× cycles for staples/high-value crops.

### Affiliate & Cooperative Scaling
- **1,000–5,000 Affiliates**: Subsidized kits/loans; microbial inoculants (+10–30% yields); agroforestry expansions.
- **Co-op Governance**: Hubs as multi-stakeholder LLCs; revenue-sharing options.

### Infrastructure Upgrades
- **Smart Roads/Rail**: Bid low/near-cost on resurfacing/cladding using RCA + tiles; integrate agrivoltaics.
- **Dam/Grid Pilots**: Selective removals with river restoration; DC microgrid expansions.

### TerraWell FreeCare Launch
- **Standalone Nonprofit**: 5–10 fixed centers + 20–50 mobiles; free urgent/primary/mental health care, DME, OTC meds, produce.
- **Donations**: Tangible goods only (§170(e)(3) deductions); hyperspectral airlocks; education modules for recipients.

Revenue drivers: Infrastructure bids (low-cost wins), premium produce branding, blockchain credits ($200–500M/hub/year potential). Self-funding solidifies; TerraWell ops funded via donations/solicitations.

## Milestones by End-2029
- 85–95% resource closure.
- Regional sewage/landfill replacement.
- First TerraWell centers serving 100K–500K.
- Affiliate flywheel: Subsidized power/water accelerating replication.
- Infrastructure leadership: 50–100 upgrade contracts.

This phase establishes Genesis as a regenerative powerhouse, with TerraWell enhancing humanitarian impact and political alignment.
