# Genesis Network  
**Detailed Multi-Trophic Ecosystem Design**  
**Core Regenerative Engine: Aquaponics + Insect + Microbial Layers**  
**Earth-Star Industries Conceptual Baseline**  
*January 17, 2026*

## Executive Overview

The **multi-trophic ecosystem** is the biological and economic heart of the Genesis Network. Deployed primarily in 40 ft high-cube pods (and scaled into vertical greenhouse towers in mature hubs), it achieves **95–99% nutrient and water closure** while producing high-value outputs: fresh greens/herbs, premium fish/prawns, insect protein (BSFL), and microbial inoculants for soil regeneration.

**Five-layer trophic structure** (top-down nutrient cascade):

1. **Primary Producers** → Plants (greens, herbs, staples, halophytes)  
2. **Primary Consumers** → Herbivorous fish (tilapia, catfish, barramundi)  
3. **Secondary Consumers / Detritivores** → Crustaceans (prawns, crayfish) + Insects (BSFL, mealworms)  
4. **Tertiary Filters** → Mollusks (mussels, clams) + Microbial consortia  
5. **Nutrient Transformers** → Biochar + plasma vitrification → ceramic feedstock

**Key performance goals per mature pod/cluster**:
- Water closure: 95–99%  
- Nutrient recycling: 92–98%  
- Annual protein output: 500–1,800 kg (fish + prawns + BSFL)  
- Greens/herbs: 2,500–8,000 kg  
- Gross revenue: $80K–$380K per single pod/year

## Detailed Trophic Layers & Resource Flows

### Layer 1: Primary Producers (Plants)
- **Species**: Lettuce, kale, basil, microgreens (fast-cycle); rice, potatoes (staples); Salicornia/kelp (saltwater pilots)  
- **Setup**: Vertical racking + floating rafts; CO₂ enrichment from biogas exhaust  
- **Cycle**: 30–90 days (greens); 120–180 days (staples)  
- **Inputs**: Fish effluent (nitrates/phosphates), LED/PAR light, microbial inoculants  
- **Outputs**: Edible biomass, trimmings → BSFL feed

### Layer 2: Primary Consumers (Fish)
- **Freshwater (80–90%)**: Nile/Blue Tilapia hybrids, Channel Catfish, Rainbow Trout (cold zones)  
- **Saltwater pilots (10–20%)**: Barramundi, Red Drum  
- **Density**: 20–60 kg/m³ final biomass (staggered cohorts)  
- **Inputs**: Plant-based feed + BSFL protein  
- **Outputs**: Edible protein, high-nutrient sludge → crustaceans/insects

### Layer 3: Secondary Consumers / Detritivores
- **Crustaceans**: Freshwater prawns (Macrobrachium rosenbergii), Red Claw Crayfish  
  - Density: 5–15 juveniles/m² + hides  
  - Role: Consume sludge/algae → premium tail meat  
- **Insects**: Black Soldier Fly Larvae (primary), mealworms/crickets  
  - Cycle: Weekly harvest  
  - Feed: Fish sludge + plant trimmings + manure  
  - Output: 35–53% protein biomass → fish/poultry feed + human-grade potential

### Layer 4: Tertiary Filters & Microbial Transformers
- **Mollusks**: Native freshwater mussels/clams  
  - Role: Filter particulates/nutrients → water polishing  
- **Microbial consortia**: Mycorrhizae, N-fixers, rhizobia, microalgae  
  - Produced in dedicated inoculant pods  
  - Boost: +10–30% plant yields; enhanced soil sequestration

### Layer 5: Final Closure & Material Loop
- **Biochar production**: Pyrolysis of trimmings/sludge → soil amendment + carbon sequestration  
- **Plasma vitrification**: Non-organic residues → toughened ceramic feedstock  
- **Outputs**: Ceramic tiles/pipes for self-replication

**Full Nutrient Cascade Flow**  
Fish waste → Crustaceans/insects breakdown organics → Mollusks filter → Plants uptake nitrates → Clean water recirculates → CO₂ from biogas → plants → repeat
