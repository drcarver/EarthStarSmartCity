# Genesis Network – Advanced Fab Contingency Strategy  
**If Internal 7 nm → 3 nm Development Is Delayed**  
**February 2026 Baseline**

## Summary

If the internal Mini-Fab roadmap for advanced nodes (7 nm → 3 nm) encounters significant delays (yield issues, tool scaling, cleanroom challenges, or regulatory hurdles pushing past 2035), the Network has **robust, tiered contingencies** to prevent disruption to mission-critical compute needs (AI inference, digital twins, robotics, badges, security systems).

The strategy prioritizes:  
- Maximizing internal mature-node output (65–28 nm) with data moat optimization  
- Preparing trusted external foundry partnerships as insurance  
- Leveraging commercial off-the-shelf (COTS) chips for non-critical workloads  
- Retaining sovereign design control at all costs  

This ensures continued hub expansion, affiliate scaling, and off-world preparation without compromising long-term independence.

## Preferred Tier 2 Partner: Samsung Foundry Texas

**Why Samsung Texas?**  
- Operational advanced nodes in Austin, Texas (3 nm GAA capability ramping 2025–2026 with CHIPS Act expansion)  
- U.S.-based → minimal export control/ITAR friction  
- Proven custom/Arm-based design support  
- CHIPS Act incentives lower cost/risk  

**Engagement Plan**  
- 2032: Non-binding LOI + test shuttle runs (28–14 nm validation)  
- 2033: Framework agreement for 7 nm pilot  
- 2034–2035: Activate full contract if internal yields <70–75% sustained  
- Cost Structure: $1.5–$4B initial tooling/access + per-wafer fees + 10–20% royalties  
- IP Protection: Genesis retains full ownership; Samsung manufactures only  

**Alternatives**  
1. TSMC Arizona (U.S. fab expansion, CHIPS-funded)  
2. Intel Foundry Services (Ohio/Arizona sites, aggressive U.S. push)  
3. GlobalFoundries (New York, strong mature-node with 12–22 nm capability)

## Strategic Recommendation

- **Primary Path**: Full commitment to internal Mini-Fab roadmap (sovereign control, lowest long-term cost, unmatched data moat advantage).  
- **Insurance Policy**: Advance Samsung Texas (or TSMC Arizona) contingency — sign LOI 2032, run test shuttles 2033, budget $1.5–$5B bridge if activated.  
- **Immediate Fallback**: Stockpile/selectively deploy COTS for non-critical systems (badges, signage, secondary robotics) to maintain momentum.  
- **Non-Negotiables**: Retain full design IP; avoid any partner demanding equity, control, or broad licensing.  

This layered, low-regret approach keeps the Network on track for advanced compute self-sufficiency while providing realistic fallbacks against unexpected delays.
