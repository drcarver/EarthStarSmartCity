# Integrated Space Expansion Strategy – Earth-Star Industries

**Objective:** Accelerate space colonization once U.S. Genesis hubs achieve self-funding, incorporating current (January 2026) realities from SpaceX Starship progress, NASA budgets, and emerging space tech. Timeline updated for delayed Starship milestones (e.g., refueling demos likely 2026–2027, uncrewed Mars 2028+). Revenue and lessons from space feed back to Earth hubs for enhanced resilience. 

**Transport Assumptions:** Rely on SpaceX Starship v3 (targeting 7–12 launches in 2026, full reusability by 2027–2028) for initial lunar/Mars transport. Lunar mass drivers (conceptual, $5–10B development) phased in later for bulk logistics. Costs recalculated: Launch to LEO ~$1,000–1,800/kg (down from prior estimates); total mission costs inflated 20–50% for realism.

**Viability Updates:** Timelines delayed 5–10 years due to Starship testing (e.g., Flight 12 Q1 2026); CapEx up 50–100% (e.g., orbital factories $5–10B each); Revenues down 20–30% initially but uplifted long-term via mining ($10–20B market by 2033). Overall project IRR adjusted to 25–35%; feasibility 7/10 (technical risks high).
