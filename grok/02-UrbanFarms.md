# Earth-Star Skyscraper Farm Network: Executive Summary and Project Plan

## Executive Summary

The Earth-Star Skyscraper Farm is a hybrid regenerative agri-food node that combines a 150 m × 150 m diameter, 15-storey controlled-environment vertical tower (265,000 m² CEA) with a contiguous 2,065 hectare (5,100 acre) greenhouse-covered regenerative horizontal farm.  
Each node feeds 20,000 people year-round with full USDA MyPlate diversity while achieving >10× land efficiency versus 2025 U.S. averages. First commercial node breaks ground 2 January 2032 in the U.S. Midwest (e.g., Illinois or Indiana) using only ceramic flat-pack kits and clear ceramic-greenhouse tiles produced by the Genesis Facility (Prompt 1).

All calorie-dense crops, orchards, grains, cotton, large livestock and poultry are grown outdoors under large-span ceramic-framed greenhouses or agrivoltaic canopies. The vertical tower is limited to high-value, low-calorie-density items (leafy greens, berries, mushrooms, aquaponics shrimp/tilapia, cultured meat, vitamins, nutraceuticals).  
Energy is supplied by anaerobic digestion + optional Sabatier (pilot-scale only) + combined-cycle methane turbines + wind + solar. Water is 98 % recycled + façade AWG + rainwater harvesting → net exporter in humid climates.
