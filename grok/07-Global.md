## 1. Strategic Overview – Sovereign Nation Adoption

Once the **Fortress West** (U.S. + Canada + Western Hemisphere partners) reaches mature scale (~2045–2050: 3,000–8,000 hubs, 200–300M people served, full internalization of essentials), the Genesis Network opens a **sovereign nation adoption pathway** for any country that formally requests participation.

**Core Rules for International Expansion**  
- Full national sovereignty preserved – no foreign ownership of land, critical infrastructure, or strategic assets  
- Country/region-specific LLCs replace sensitive U.S.-centric ones  
- Cultural & religious accommodations mandatory (halal/kosher processing, indigenous governance, local dietary/ethical norms)  
- Technology transfer limited to non-sensitive domains (tiles, aquaponics, biogas, ceramics, basic robotics)  
- Sensitive domains (advanced semiconductors, rare gases, sovereign AI/data moat, advanced DAC) remain U.S./NATO-controlled or licensed under strict bilateral agreements  
- Model: Licensed joint ventures, royalty-based kit exports, local co-ops, and sovereign adaptation funds (revenue-share from global kit sales)

**Non-Transferable Core Sensitive LLCs** (remain U.S./NATO-controlled)  
- TerraFab (Electronics & Appliances)  
- Industrial Gases (including rare gases)  
- Robotics & Automation (advanced swarm/control)  
- Data Moat & Digital Twin  
- Direct Air Capture & Mineralization (advanced modular)

## 4. Key Adaptation Principles for Sovereign Nations

- **Cultural/Religious**: Mandatory halal/kosher lines where requested; indigenous governance in Americas/Oceania/Africa; dietary & ethical norms (e.g., vegetarian emphasis in India)  
- **Sovereignty**: Local ownership of non-sensitive LLCs; data residency laws enforced via local twins  
- **Technology Transfer**: Licensed kits for tiles, aquaponics, biogas, ceramics; sensitive tech (TerraFab, advanced gases) via bilateral security agreements only  
- **Funding**: Sovereign adaptation funds seeded from global kit royalties (5–15% revenue share)
