## Executive Summary

The Genesis Network's 50-year roadmap (2026–2076) integrates NASA/ISS-derived technologies (e.g., closed-loop life support, radiation shielding, robotics) into regenerative Earth hubs, creating feedback loops where space experiments refine tech for terrestrial use, and Earth-scale optimizations advance lunar/Mars settlements. This symbiotic cycle accelerates innovation, reduces costs through data moat/AI iterations, and ensures self-funding via revenue from adapted tech (e.g., AWG from ISS water recovery, sodium-ion from space batteries).

NASA/ISS Tech Marked: Derived tech explicitly noted (e.g., *NASA/ISS: Closed-Loop ECLSS*). Feedback Loops: Space → Earth (e.g., ISS microgravity data optimizes hub microbial inoculants); Earth → Space (e.g., hub sequestration boosts inform Mars regolith farming).

Recomputed Financials (network-wide, $B): Total CAPEX 2026–2076 $100–$250B (Earth $15–$35B, space pilots $85–$215B offset by data/mining revenue); Revenue 2050 $500–$2,000B/year (Earth 80%, space 20%); NPV (8% discount) $5–$15T; IRR 30–45%; break-even 2032 Earth, 2055 space. Viability uplift +15–20% from NASA synergies (faster R&D via public data/partnerships).
