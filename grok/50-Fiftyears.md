## Overview

This 50-year roadmap integrates NASA/ISS-derived technologies (marked **NASA/ISS**) into the Genesis Network's progression from Earth-based regenerative hubs to lunar/Mars settlements. Core feedback loops:
- **Earth → Space**: Proven closed-loop systems (ECLSS-inspired) scaled for off-world testing.
- **Space → Earth**: Harsh-environment data (radiation, low-gravity yields) feeds Earth digital twins → efficiency gains.
- **Bidirectional Flywheel**: Space experiments refine Earth hubs; Earth scale funds space replication.

Financials assume aggressive self-funding (greens/tiles/biogas early); space phase requires public-private synergies post-2050.

**NASA/ISS Core Contributions**:
- **ECLSS/BLSS**: Closed environmental/life support systems → water/air/food recycling (95–99% closure).
- **CELSS**: Controlled Ecological Life Support → hydroponics/aquaponics foundations.
- **AWG/Regolith Processing**: Atmospheric water generation + lunar slag → ceramics/oxygen.
- **Radiation/Habitat Tech**: ISS data informs shielding (ceramic bladders) and low-g farming.

## Financial Projections Summary

- **2026–2050 (Earth Phase)**: CAPEX $250–750B; Revenue ramps to $500–$2T/year; fully self-funded by 2030.
- **2051–2100 (Space Phase)**: CAPEX $18–90T (public-private essential); Revenue $2–10T/year long-term (resources/habitats).
- **IRR**: Earth 35–55%; Space 20–40% (high risk).
- **NPV (7% discount)**: Earth $300–$800B; Space $1–$5T (conservative synergies).

The roadmap leverages NASA/ISS tech for closure/resilience, with bidirectional loops accelerating innovation across Earth and space.
