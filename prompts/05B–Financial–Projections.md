## Objective

Provide the **single, consolidated financial truth** for the Genesis Network: how it becomes **cash-flow positive quickly**, **self-funds all growth**, and scales to national/global coverage without long-term public funding reliance.

All numbers are recalculated for the pruned scope (8 core pods, ~12 tiles, ~10 kits, 12 LLCs) and conservative-to-aggressive scenarios. Focus is **2027–2040** (U.S. coverage); 2040+ treated as directional.

## Key Assumptions (Pruned & Grounded)

- Self-funding core: No sustained public grants after 2030–2032  
- Early revenue drivers: Greens/eggs/honey (Month 1–3), biogas tipping, tile sales (Month 2–6)  
- Mature margins: 65–85% (automation + closure)  
- CAPEX offsets: 20–35% from CHIPS/IRA/EPA brownfield grants (short-term only)  
- Affiliate flywheel: 10–25% of revenue recycled into loans/kits  
- Scenarios: Conservative (slow adoption), Base (realistic), Aggressive (optimal execution)  
- Discount rate: 7% for NPV; inflation ignored for simplicity

## Self-Funding Path – Key Milestones

- **Month 1–3**: First hubs cash-positive via greens/eggs/honey/biogas tipping/tile sales ($0.5–5M/hub)  
- **Year 1 (2027–2028)**: 5–50 hubs generate $5–80B cumulative revenue → funds expansion  
- **Year 3 (2029–2030)**: Network-wide self-funding threshold reached  
- **Year 5 (2031–2032)**: Affiliate flywheel self-accelerating; grants no longer needed  
- **Year 10 (2035–2036)**: Cities/towers drive exponential internal revenue  

**Risk-Adjusted Note**: Conservative scenario assumes 30–50% slower adoption → still self-funding by 2032. Aggressive scenario hits escape velocity by 2029.
